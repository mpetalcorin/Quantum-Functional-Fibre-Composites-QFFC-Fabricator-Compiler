# Model Card ﾑ QFFC Surrogate Models (Recipe ? Quantum Readiness)## Model overviewThis repository trains **surrogate models** that map controllable manufacturing recipe setpoints to:- a composite quantum-readiness score (`qffc_score`),- individual quantum proxies (NV ODMR contrast/linewidth, g?(0), Er storage time proxy, TFLN Vｹ甅 proxy, insertion loss proxy),- and a binary QC label (ﾒquantum-readyﾓ) derived from explicit thresholds.Primary model family:- RandomForestRegressor (scikit-learn)Purpose:- Fast screening of candidate recipes and identification of stable process corridors for QFFC manufacturing.**Important:** These models are trained on simulated data and are not validated predictors of real-world performance.---## Intended use### Intended- Demonstrate a manufacturing-first QC + optimisation workflow for QFFC development.- Explore how recipe variables could influence defect formation and quantum-relevant outcomes.- Generate candidate recipes for subsequent testing (in simulation or later in experiments).- Provide a template that can be re-trained on experimental metrology once available.### Not intended- Clinical, safety-critical, or production decisions.- Direct prediction of real quantum device performance without calibration.- Claims of physical accuracy for the underlying mechanistic relationships.---## InputsControllable recipe setpoints (features):- pH- ionic strength (mM)- temperature (｡C)- silica precursor concentration (mM)- residence time (s)- draw speed (m/min)- tension proxy (a.u.)- mixing quality (0ﾐ1)- scaffold robustness (0ﾐ1)---## OutputsContinuous targets:- `qffc_score`- `nv_odmr_contrast`- `nv_odmr_linewidth_mhz`- `g2_0`- `er_storage_us`- `tfln_vpiL_vcm`- `insert_loss_db_per_cm`Derived binary label:- `quantum_ready` (defined by an explicit QC gate in the notebook)---## Training data- Fully synthetic dataset generated by the notebook.- Simulated host outcomes include geometry, roughness/scattering proxies, and defect events per metre.- Quantum readouts are simulated as monotonic degradations with increasing defects/roughness/scattering, anchored to literature-scale priors.See `DataSheet.md` for details.---## EvaluationThe notebook reports:- MAE and R? on a held-out test split for each target.- Feature importance for `qffc_score`.Caveat:- Good predictive performance on simulated data does not imply real-world validity.---## Limitations- Mechanistic mapping is phenomenological, not first-principles physics.- No domain shift handling (simulation ? experiment) is implemented.- ﾒQuantum readinessﾓ thresholds are heuristic and should be recalibrated with real metrology.- Correlations are shaped by how the simulator is constructed.---## Ethical and safety considerations- This work is purely computational and does not provide instructions for harmful activity.- Do not use for any safety-critical operational decision-making.- Any future wet-lab translation should follow institutional safety, ethics, and compliance standards.---## How to improve and
